The Merchant City Inn - BOOK DIRECT FOR BEST RATES!
May 30 2022

At last my date of departure has arrived! \240I’m very excited and glad I planned this way, as I got an excellent night’s sleep after the 12-hr ferry ride followed by 3 hours on the train! \240The Merchant City Inn is plain but clean and comfortable. \240Glasgow seems such a wild place: yelling of drunks in the streets at 3 am, solicitation from a window across the way when I peeked out to see a red firelight in their window. \240A nice cuppa, and I’ll duly exercise with my band in preparation for a long day’s journey. \240I’m hoping to find my favorite serum in the duty-free shop at Reykjavik. \240But at least, a bottle of good single-malt for Tom for caring for Lilly all this time.

Even this place has its own stone spiral staircase. \240They may not be “code,” but they’re easy access with a heavy suitcase.

View across from my room this morning. \240Many brownstones here, reminds me of Montreal.

I used to live on S. Frederick St in Arlington for a time…

George Square, very easy to catch airport express bus.

Doric columns on the Bank of Scotland. \240The old architectural details are very beautiful, including entire standing women on cornices, faces, all made of plaster by artisans of the past. \240Sometimes, though, a company just buys an old block, razes it, and builds a new, mostly glass, modern structure with zero charm.

Final glimpses of Glasgow…

I like these apartments; always wanted to live on a round corner. \240One of my riding students in Montreal, Charley Russell, the handsome young man I borrowed from in order to buy my first horse, lived in Westmount, in Montreal, in just such a round corner apartment. \240How do I remember this? \240(I only remembered his first name a week later.)

“Hekla Aurora”’s almost ready to roll!
