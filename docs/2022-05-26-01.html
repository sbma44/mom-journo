Unst Baltasound
May 26 2022

I can’t get used to the morning sun pouring into my skylight before 4 am every day! \240I’m so far north! \240Sunset isn’t until 10 pm…

I think I can make it up to the Hagdale Horse Mill. \240It’s a 2+ hour walk round trip. \240I love the peacefulness here, and the sun is shining. \240It would be good not to spend much money today, but I think I will get that smoked mackerel on my way back.

I just wanted this photo of the mill to lead, as it was a destination well-achieved, despite the wind and the rain today! \240(Hey, Ho, hey ho—that old song keeps popping into my head due to the constant variance of weather here!)

I was wondering just when those large poppies would bloom here along the walkway from my sunroom to the road.

Just as soon as I set out, the rains set in, and I was just passing Jim’s place. \240He invited me to share his garage with him while the clouds passed by. \240This was so nice of him, as I was definitely getting soaked right away! \240He amazed me, though, in speaking to me about the dreadful school shooting in Texas that just happened. \240I find many Scots are completely up-to-date on American politics. \240Jim held forth about the Democrats and the Republicans, as well as knowing quite a bit about the strength of our gun lobby. \240It’s such a crime. \240As President Biden said, “How can we live with this complete madness?”

Marsh marigolds.

Spring squill? \240Of course, it’s wonderful to correctly name every bird and flower, but only humans have created these names and made naming things important. \240More importantly, God created each bird and flower for us to enjoy, and their names mean very little, actually. \240I just love it that, wherever I walk in Shetland, I see myriads of birds, all very busy in their springtime pursuits of courting, mating, nest building, home discovering, foraging, and flying! \240And I love almost as much the proliferation of beautiful wildflowers just about everywhere you look!

I decided to go up to the store Mary pointed out to me yesterday. \240En route, I saw this Church of Scotland, and its doors were open. \240As soon as I entered, though, I saw that the funeral of a Mr Nisbet was going to take place shortly, and mourners were already arriving, so not wishing to disturb, I left.

This store really did have everything, and was better-stocked than the other two. \240It had a very good selection of books, just as Mary’d told me. \240I may return for a souvenir or two, for tomorrow’s my last day in Shetland.

I just had to return for a frontal interior shot of Bobby’s Bus Shelter. \240The whole thing is done up for Queen Elizabeth’s Platinum Jubilee: “Lovely Jubbly!”

And a selfie on the throne!

At last I found the turn for the Hagdale Horse Mill. \240It runs right past the Balta Bar, seen in the background.

Here you can see some ruins of this chromite mill.

It’s the only original horse-drawn mill in existence. The chromite ore here was precious and very rare.

A good view of the Hagdale Horse Mill.

These daffodils standing alone near the energy farm were so fragrant! \240They were managing to stay intact despite the strong winds. I wanted to photograph them because they’re some of the latest daffodils of 2022, if not the very last. \240After all, it’s almost June!

I spent about 5 hours total walking around, and went through several rain showers, one that felt like being pelted with sharp hail, and winds so strong I had to turn around—several times!

A view of the Baltasound Pier with a few cows grazing. \240One sees far more sheep and lambs here, as well as ponies, than cows.

Linda’s veggie burger was really delicious! \240I did stop in and bought the smoked mackerel, though. That will furnish me two more good breakfasts. \240Glad to see her legacy, like Paul Newman’s, still exists.

I ran into Alice, the niece of my hosts, on my way back. \240I was glad to share a few words with her, but it’s been good to be as private as I’ve been here. \240It has been very good for my soul. \240I’ve had much relaxation and time for prayer and reading in between my hikes. \240In the evenings, I’ve finally managed to watch British TV, even though somehow I turned off the color feature, so it’s all in black & white, somehow appropriate.

The distances here are always farther than one thinks. \240A so-called “2+ hour” hike round trip turned into twice that today. \240Haven’t decided what I’ll do tomorrow for my last day in Shetland, but my poor feet and toes are telling me, “not too much, please.”

10 pm sunset through my skylight. \240Good night after a cup of warm milk.
