Ordaal House
May 24 2022

I’m glad I’ve hired a taxi later today so I can get out to the Hermaness National Nature Reserve. \240After the past two days of walking, I can’t kid myself that I’m in great shape for more walking along the roads. \240I will do some hiking at Hermaness, but hopefully it will be on soft ground and boardwalks, not as hard as asphalt. \240I’m looking forward to this! \240All the birds are nesting now: puffins, arctic terns, Great Skua (a/k/a “Bonxie”), Gannet, Razorbill, Fulmar, Shag, Guillemot, Kittiwake, Black Guillemot, Gulls, and perhaps I’ll also see the delicate Edmonston’s Chickweed, which grows only here. \240I’m doing my best with the myriad Shetland guidebooks here in the house to familiarize myself with these birds and plants so I’ll be able to identify them. \240It’s pretty cold out today, very cloudy.

The orca whales have been spotted close by, so I might see them, too! \240And otters.

First glimpse of Hermaness!

It is a lovely boardwalk constructed of recycled plastic. \240What good usage!

Any guesses on whose bones these are?

It’s a sheer drop down….I don’t go too near the edge!

It’s afar off, but all the white you see are birds! \240You can smell the guano, but with the winds whipping, it’s not overpowering.

No tammie norries (puffins). \240I’ll keep searching for them….

It almost makes one dizzy to follow the flight of the gannets. \240They’re huge birds with 6’ wingspans. \240The majestic views here on the most northern tip of the UK are spectacular. \240Words cannot express. \240I’m so blessed to be able to witness these cliffs and sea birds!

The strength in this feather is seen here. \240The winds up here are enormous. \240The birds must be strong not to be thrust against the cliff sides.

Gannets roosting.

Birds are flying EVERYWHERE! \240Sheep and lambs go right on the edge. \240Lambs often sleep in burrows they find, sheltered from the wind.

Guidebooks ask that you don’t wear synthetic trousers: too slick. \240You just might slip off the edge here. \240I must say, it was scary, and you’re quite alone. \240I did pass some people, and some were solo travelers such as myself, but nevertheless!

An Arctic Skua. \240My hike was about 4 hours round trip. \240My ankle just about done!

A shelter was just built at this area. \240It broke the wind while I waited for my taxi. \240Only Jamieson’s serves Unst, but Lee Jamieson is very kind and helpful. All the buses are also Jamieson drivers, and he’s got cars for rent. \240I’m just not up for it. \240I’m so terrible at backing, and here, with so many single-lane roads, you’re often called upon to back up! \240Lee explained how I can find the puffins (fingers crossed) at Sumburgh head on my last day in Shetland. \240My ferry will get into Lerwick at 7 am, and I won’t need to board the big ferry to Aberdeen until 7 pm, so I’ll have time.

I keep looking for otters, too. \240They’re supposedly in Baltasound, but I haven’t seen any yet. \240I kind of wish I’d already found a Shetland sweater because it’s just cold most of the time! \240I brought two cashmere sweaters, not realizing that I’d REALLY need to wear them EVERY day, thankful to have brought a tee to wear underneath, AND my Ariat puffy vest under my windbreaker.
