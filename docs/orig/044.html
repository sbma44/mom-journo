Baltasound Pier
May 27 2022

Yes, I polished off the last of that home-baked Millionaire’s Shortbread today! \240If you’ve never tried this delicacy, you really must!

I had to wait awhile for heavy rains to cease prior to taking my final day’s long walk around Baltasound. I basically know what I’m looking at across the sound now, having walked nearly the entire shoreline a few times. What strikes me most about this landscape, in general, is the quality of light throughout the day. \240The weather’s so varied, so quickly changing, and so is the light. \240To be surrounded so completely by birds and sheep, water and stones, shells and walls. \240It is Shetland. \240Amazing!

This little one’s all alone in a paddock; I wish the owners would get a companion for this pony. \240Even a sheep. \240Equines are herd animals; they don’t like to be alone.

I made it down to the Pier, and found a monument dedicated to the Swedish fishermen who came to Baltasound and founded a Swedish church here. \240Apparently there have been numerous gales here which destroyed this church as well as many other buildings that stood on the pier. \240Perhaps this is one reason the boat trip I’d hoped to take from Baltasound Pier no longer exists.

View up from the pier. \240Here in Scotland, you never see a plastic bag or trash on the loose. \240I was in a grocery store, and asked how much a bag would cost. \240“10 pounds,” was the very sensible answer! \240I’ve been with people shopping, and they either have bags (as I do at home), or they just put all the loose products in the back seat! \240We should learn from the Scots!

After the rain and wind, a beautiful sunny day! \240When I’m walking here, often the lyric from the hymn “Lead, Kindly Light” comes to mind: “O’er moor and fen, O’er crag and hillside til the night is gone…”

I love the pastoral views here with stone walls and sheep everywhere!

Farewell to Baltasound.

The marsh marigolds.

I returned to the St John’s Kirk and took a closer look at this “Fish of Genesis” sculpture. \240A definite contrast of ancient with modern.

Peering through a window, I could see simple cushions atop wooden pews, and a simple cross in stained glass in front. \240There was also a Peace Pole in the church garden where I read its history and provenance, starting with a Japanese man who reacted to the use of the atomic bomb on Hiroshima with a life of prayer and meditation for peace. \240Would that the human heart could bring about peace. \240Lord, please bring us peace, not the slaughter of innocent children at school. \240I don’t know whether I can continue to live in my country.

I returned to Henderson’s Grocery Store and bought 2 Shetland calendars for 2023, a sweet children’s book entitled, “Benny-benny Seawater: The First Day of the Summer Holidays,” which describes a young boy going off to his uncle’s croft, where just about everything he does my granddaughters will never have heard of! \240I also bought some oatcakes. \240I needed to get a few “ homespun” souvenirs.

The ram had beautiful horns!

I would like to keep sheep. \240I would make an excellent shepherdess!

I took a closer look at this old abandoned ruin of a house, imagining who must have loved living here, once upon a time.

My favorite ewe with her black & brown lambies. I called her Lolly. They have a field all to themselves, too.

6 pm Farewell to Baltasound. \240Many handsome boats in the sound today.

Ah, Baltasound! \240
