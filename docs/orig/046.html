4WVM+6V Aberdeen, UK
May 29 2022

Docking at Aberdeen. The many smokers on deck. \240A good continental breakfast under my belt. Such welcome, wonderful

kindness on board from the crew.

Heck! \240I missed the sighting of the Orca whale this morning as we docked. \240Only later did I make the acquaintance of two intrepid octogenarians, Jean and Mairi, whom I’d seen earlier on my ferry to Orkney, who told me about it, and it was their first and only sighting! \240They spent a week at Sumburgh at the hotel, and had had a wonderful time, walking with their walking poles. \240I really admire them, traveling everywhere with their walkers.

I like all the Viking emblems on board the massive “Hjaltsland” ferry.

NorthLink ferries!

We had a perfectly glorious, full rainbow for our arrival. \240We walked to the train station together, but as it’s Sunday, not much was open early. Jean & Mairi. \240Lovely ladies!

I must be in the right place! \240Aberdeen, here I come!

And a bookended Costa coffee. \240Absolutely delicious! \240But now I’ve a bit over an hour to wait for my train to Glasgow. \240I bid Jean & Mairi farewell.

Having to wait for my train, getting used to the cold, enjoying the miraculous life of St Columba.

Mairi let me take a photo of this photo her son took of a “tammie norrie.”

At long last, my train to Glasgow is moving. \240I’m so tired! \240My hotel has an early check-in, which I’m sure to take advantage of.

It’s a cloudy day with some patches of sun. \240As we come to the town of Montrose, I’m reminded that the countryside here a reminiscent of my own Shenandoah area…

Very nice to be in 1st Class, although I hadn’t intended…

Tea was complimentary on the train: Lovely!

I must have passed 5 golf courses already; its being Sunday, there are hordes of golfers!

And many dressed to the nines.

Leaving Dundee, reputedly the home town of Margaret Croll, my grandmother. \240What a contrast to the pure wild of Shetland! \240One begins to see large trees, beautifully cultivated gardens, and graceful thoroughbred horses in the fields.

Perth. \240While my train traversed the countryside, I saw many deer, little red deer with antlers, and beautiful horses in meadows, jumps laid out in outdoor rings, definitely horse country here!

Glasgow, at last.

My Merchant City Inn is right opposite this!

Another “real” unicorn, this one in Glasgow. \240My impression is of a very old city, with many poor and many affluent people. \240I stopped at an outdoor “bodega,” ordered a Tennent’s and the fresh mussels. \240What great taste! \240I couldn’t leave this wonderful Scotland without a taste of those mussels. \240They were done to perfection!

Typical Glasgow scene as I head back to my hotel, having found some small gifts for Mary & Izabelle.

This photo is for Celeste, who still works at TJ’s!

I am thankful that Merchant City Inn has great hot water, lovely clean towels. \240I took a shower, and am watching the ridiculous “Voyage to the Bottom of the Sea.” \240I didn’t recall Frankie Avalon in the cast.

All of Britain is ready for Her Majesty’s Platinum Jubilee! \240Many days off this coming week.
