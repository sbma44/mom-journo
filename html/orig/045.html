Toft - Ulsta Ferry
May 28 2022

This is the puffin photo Mairi’s son took. \240I really like it!

I’m up early with the birds and the cold, cloudy dawn. \240This room heats up quickly with the radiator, fortunately. \240I’m excited about beginning my long journey back home. \240I’ve been away so long, six weeks! I slept better because I located a shade I could pull down on the skylight. \240Got it open now, though, full of thanks to God for all who’ve been praying for me during this pilgrimage of mine. \240Glad my feet and ankle have held out admirably. I do take extreme care to keep them clean, make sure my socks are, too, and fit my shoes on just the right tension of the laces. \240I’ve walked so much, the shoes are nearly worn out!

I’ve been listening to the wind in the skylight all night long. \240The wind off the sound never ceases, and it’s fun watching the birds ride the winds around here. \240I wonder if it ever warms up! \240It’s been pretty cold every day, with the exception of brief bursts of afternoon sun. \240The grazing is perfect for the sheep; they seem all fat and happy.

I want to read just a few more pages of “Full Tilt.” \240In a way, Dervla Murphy is keeping a journal just like mine, only by hand and with no pictures. Her memoir of riding her bike, Roz, which she always refers to as “she,” is dated by place, and she’s faithfully creating a funny and interesting record as she travels through Afghanistan and Pakistan. \240Plucky!

At the bus stop, I met a young man who said he’s not very interested in coming to the US because of the absurdity of our gun laws and all this murder. \240It’s been hard, too, to have Charlottesville be recognized solely for the Alt-Right White Supremacists consistently, when I tell people where I’m from. \240I don’t blame them; I may change the place I say. \240But isn’t it difficult to be ashamed of the country my father was so proud to immigrate to?

I’m on the ferry now, moving slowly across the waters from Ulsta to Toth(?). The bus driver has gone to “the passenger saloon” and left the door wide open. \240It’s so cold!

Starting out for the first ferry, from Belmont to Toft.

There’s so much empty land up here, all peat.

There’s the next ferry.

The Viking Bus Station in Lerwick. \240Lerwick means “Muddy Bay” in old Norse.

Scenes of Lerwick as I head out to Sumburgh by bus.

The lighthouse at Sumburgh, the southernmost point on Shetland. \240Here is where Lee Jamieson told me I’d have a good chance of seeing puffins.

My first tammie norrie! \240(Puffin)!

Puffins are in these pictures; you have to look carefully, as I also did on the hillside!

I wasn’t able to get too close to them,,but they were definitely fun to watch as they went in and out of their burrows, flapped their wings, scratched, took off in flight. \240I saw a couple bring catch back in their beaks. \240The reason they’re endangered now is because the warming of the oceans has reduced the number of sand eels, their basic food.

I kept trying to get a better photo!

See them now? \240They’re so funny, darting in and out of their burrows.

They’re jet black; that’s one reason I didn’t like the gift photo, but glad to have it nevertheless.

In order to see the puffins, I had to climb over that stile and sit on top of the stone wall here. \240The gate was too hard to open and shut.

The coffee house on Sumburgh has this panoramic view! \240 Plus excellent desserts!

Julian Allen & I happened to share a table. \240Turned out, he’s been coming here for many years. He shared a prize photo.

Sometimes, rarely, the Orca whales show themselves here in the area. \240I thought I’d see them today, but alas, …..no.

The hillside colors were spectacular, with the different colors of rocks as well as the pretty pink “sea thrift.”

See the seal at the bottom, basking in the whirlpools?

Julian’s Puffin. \240A great shot of a great bird!

Return to Lerwick after a very exciting excursion to Sumburgh Head by bus and taxi. \240Well worth it! \240I’m indebted to Lee Jamieson on Unst for the information on how to put this excursion together and still catch the ferry to Aberdeen.

We are on our way to Aberdeen. \240While in Lerwick, I managed to find Ninian’s, the original Shetland wool shop for sweaters, and bought a beautiful brown one for my souvenir. \240I also bought some Orkney-fashioned labradorite stud earrings for Beth. \240I hope she’ll like them.

Once on board, I was grateful they were serving a first-rate haddock fish & chips with peas. \240I was so hungry after hiking all over Sumburgh today looking for tammie norrie!

I’m going to hunt down my recliner. \240Alas, I forgot to take my medications and toothbrush, toothpaste, from my suitcase before stowing it. \240I will remember to get these things just as soon as we get to Aberdeen.
